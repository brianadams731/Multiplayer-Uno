Start Playing Now!

To begin a game of UNO, first register your user by clicking the Register button in the navbar.
You will be automatically logged in upon successful registration and redirected to the user dashboard.
From here, you can either choose to create a game or join an existing game from a list of available game
lobbies. Some lobbies will require a password, so if you are looking to join one of these lobbies, be sure
to request the password from the game lobby creator!

Game Rules

At the start of every game, all players will be dealt five cards face-down. The first player will be granted
the option of playing any card they wish! In case you wish to remind yourself on whose turn it is, the
list of players on the top left of the screen will have a single player name highlighted to show
whose turn it is currently! To play a valid card on your turn, you must ensure the card you wish to
play matches the current top card's color or value (ex. if the current top card is a red 2, then you will
be able to play any card that is red or a 2 of any color). UNO also has special cards with special
effects. Click the next page in the bottom button list to learn more about these special cards!

Special Cards

When you are playing, you will notice there exists 5 different special cards outside of the generic 0-9 numeric cards. Here's what they do:

  1. Draw Two Cards: If you play the draw two card, you will force the player whose turn it is next to draw two cards to their hand.
  2. Skip Cards: If you play the skip card, the player whose turn it is next will have their turn skipped!
  3. Reverse Cards: If you play the reverse card, the turn order will be reversed!
  4. Wild Cards: If you play the wild card, you can choose which color you want to be played from the next turn onwards! The wild card is
    unique in that you can play it disregarding the need to match the color or value of the card currently at the top of the discard pile!
  5. Wild Draw Four Cards: If you play the wild draw four card, you can choose which color you want to be played
    from the next turn onwards AND force the following player to draw four cards to their hand! The
    wild draw four card is similar to the wild card in that you can play it disregarding the need to
    match the color or value of the card currently at the top of the discard pile!
